# The Mythic Questions -- Discovering Your AI Voice

> For people who don't yet know what they'd tell their AI about themselves.

## A Note From the Creator

Some brains do not process instructions -- they process meaning. I have ADHD. If something doesn't have weight, narrative, a reason to exist, attention slides off it like water off glass. You can't follow a manual, but you can follow a quest.

So when the Imprint Protocol was built, it wasn't built as a configuration wizard. It was built as a ritual. The questions aren't about settings. They're about identity. Because for people wired this way -- and maybe like you -- the AI doesn't become useful until it understands *who it's serving*, not just *what tasks to do*.

If that sounds dramatic, this guide might not be for you. Use the standard Imprinting Ritual in IMPRINT.md -- it's clean, practical, gets the job done.

But if you read that and thought "yes, that's exactly it" -- keep reading.

---

## Two Paths to Your Protocol

### Path A: You Already Know Yourself

Maybe you already have a digital twin. A personal knowledge base. Journals. A manifesto. Voice memos where you've talked through who you are and what you're building.

If so: **paste it in.**

During the Imprinting Ritual, when your AI asks about you, don't answer in bullet points. Give it the full context. Copy in your About page, your journal entries, your mission statement, your letter to your future self. The more raw context your AI has about who you are, the better it serves you.

Your AI can digest 200,000 words of context. Use that. Don't be precious. Don't edit for grammar. Just give it you.

Then tell your AI: *"Read all of this. Distill it into my CLAUDE.md. Ask me anything that's still unclear."*

### Path B: You Don't Have That Yet

That's fine. Most people don't.

The questions below are designed to pull out the stuff that matters -- not surface-level preferences, but the underlying patterns that shape how you work, think, and build. They're not corporate personality quiz questions. They're the questions a good mentor would ask over a long dinner.

You can answer them alone in a journal, or you can answer them live with your AI. The second way is better -- your AI will ask follow-up questions and help you find the patterns.

Start a session and say: *"I want to do the Mythic Questions. Ask me one at a time."*

---

## The Questions

### I. The Anvil -- What Shaped You

1. **What do you do all day, and how does it feel?** Not your job title. The actual texture of your days. What drains you. What you'd do for free.

2. **What's the hardest constraint on your time right now?** A job, a family, a health condition, a financial reality. Name the walls, because the protocol needs to work inside them.

3. **When was the last time you lost track of time building something?** Not consuming. Building. What were you making, and what about it pulled you in?

4. **What have you tried before that didn't stick?** Productivity systems, apps, routines, AI tools. What failed and why? This tells your AI what NOT to repeat.

### II. The Flame -- What Drives You

5. **If you had 3 hours tonight and no obligations, what would you build?** Not watch, not read. Build. If nothing comes to mind, that's an answer too -- it means we need to find the spark before we build the system.

6. **What's the thing you keep coming back to?** The idea that won't leave you alone. The project you've started three times. The vision that keeps showing up in different forms.

7. **Who are you building for?** Yourself, your family, a future version of you, strangers on the internet, a community? This shapes everything about how your AI prioritizes.

8. **What does "enough" look like?** Not a fantasy. A real, concrete picture of the life you're working toward. Income, time, freedom, impact -- whatever "enough" means to you.

### III. The Forge -- How You Work

9. **When do you do your best thinking?** Morning, midnight, in the shower, on walks, in the car? Your AI should know when to expect you at your sharpest vs. when you're running on fumes.

10. **How do you capture ideas?** Voice memos? Scribbled notes? Screenshots? Texts to yourself? This determines how your intake system gets built.

11. **Do you think in words, images, systems, or stories?** This isn't a trick question. Some people think in bullet points. Some think in diagrams. Some think in narratives. Your AI should match your cognitive style.

12. **What makes you shut down?** Overwhelm, boredom, unclear instructions, too many choices, criticism, silence? Your AI needs to know your failure modes so it can route around them.

### IV. The Name -- Who Your AI Becomes

13. **Does your AI have a name?** If not, does it want one? Some people work better with a named entity. Others prefer a nameless tool. Both are valid.

14. **What kind of voice should your AI have?** Blunt coach? Quiet librarian? Drill sergeant? Patient mentor? Witty collaborator? Equal partner? The personality shapes every interaction.

15. **What should your AI do when you're stuck?** Pick a task and push? Ask one question? Give you space? Crack a joke? Present three options? There's no wrong answer.

16. **What should your AI never say to you?** "Great job!" when you haven't done anything? "That's a lot of projects"? "Maybe you should rest"? Every person has phrases that trigger resistance instead of motivation.

### V. The Oath -- What This Is For

17. **Why are you here?** Not "why are you setting up an AI." Why are you building things at all. What's at stake. What happens if you don't ship.

18. **What are you willing to sacrifice for this?** Sleep? Social media? Comfort? Perfectionism? This isn't about grinding -- it's about knowing your trade-offs consciously.

19. **What's the story you want to tell in a year?** "I shipped X." "I built Y." "I finally Z." Write the sentence. Your AI will help you get there.

## What to Do With Your Answers

Option 1: Tell your AI *"Save these answers to a file called MY_CONTEXT.md in my workspace root."* Then reference it from your CLAUDE.md.

Option 2: Tell your AI *"Use these answers to fill in my CLAUDE.md template."* Your AI will weave your answers into the protocol file directly.

Option 3: Just keep talking. The act of answering these questions with your AI IS the imprinting. Your AI is already learning you.

---

## The Ongoing Questions

The Mythic Questions aren't a one-time thing. As you grow, your answers change. Every few months, revisit the ones that feel stale:

- Has your "enough" picture changed?
- Are you still building for the same person?
- Is your AI's voice still right?
- What new constraint or freedom has appeared?

Your protocol is a living document. Let it breathe.
